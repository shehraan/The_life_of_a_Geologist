The life of a Geologist

Job opportunities for a geologist in America, Canada, and Ontario

  1. In America, 77.80% of geologists are male while 22.20% are female. The share of male geologists has increased by 2% from 2010. Additionally, in 2010, about 83% of geologists were white, while 6% were asian, another 6% were Hispanic or Latino and 3% were Black or African American. Through recent data, it is evident that this trend did not chnage and the numbers have stayed [nearly] the same. The geologist unemployment rate has also decreased over time, from 2.4% in 2010 to 1.4% most recently.   2. In Canada, geology is an uncommon career path, it is a tough job to find data for. Instead of a geologist in particular, a more common career that is closely related within the geologist field, is an environmental scientist. The difference between the two is that an environmental geologist specializes in discovering the structure, composition, and processes of our planet in order to find natural resources and find ways to prevent environmental damage caused by humans. Meanwhile, geology is much broader and also includes other careers in geology such as geological engineering. Nation-wide, the job outlook of an environmental geologist is fair across the board. However, exceptions are made for 2 provinces: Saskatchewan and Alberta. In Saskatchewan, the job outlook fairs very well and it would be the best province for a geologist to find work in. Meanwhile, Alberta has the poorest job outlook for a geologist and should not be in consideration when choosing a province to move to, for your job.
  3. In Ontario, environmental geologists earn a median income of $47.60 per hour. The income range is between $31.25 and $62.50. The job outlook is good for geologists in Ontario in the next two years as there is employment growth which will bring many new professions. This will help compensate for the recently unemployed workers who previously worked in this job.

Learning opportunities

    Augmented reality is a technology that is enabling new experiential learning opprtunities in the field of geology. Through augmented reality, students can use constructs and sandboxes to effectively train themselves in the field. These sandboxes and constructs would allow for a realistic simulation of earth's processes which would provide for a neat demonstration and can be included in tutorials that provide experiential learning.

    Another option for students that want to gain experiential learning in geology, is an internship. Internships will allow these students to test out their career path, such as by collecting substances, testing soil samples, and analyzing the data. Internships are also advantageous in that they allow students to earn money alongside doing the experiential learning.

    A student can also attend virtual workshops, such as the ones offered by Mining Matters. A workshop like this, can allow students to have an attempt at developing their reasoning and critical thinking skills while also reinforcing any prior knowledge.

Post-secondary programs for a geologist

While there are many post-secondary programs in geology, the main ones that I will be taking a look at, is the environmental geoscience program offered by the Trent University, the University of Toronto, and the University of Manitoba

  1. Trent Unviversity

    2. For admissions, the Trenth University requires an six grade 12 4U or 4M courses, including MHF4U or MCV4U, SCH4U and the ENG4U course with at least a mark of 60%. In addition, applicants must also have an Ontario Secondary School Diploma, as well as a 75% average mark. The program is heavily based on experiential learning through hands-on lab exercises and work placements. This program can set you on a strong path for your career and give you the experience needed to advance as a worker. However, the tuition fees for this University are hidden and can only be discovered by speaking directly to their support.

  2. Unviversity of Toronto

    3. The University of Toronto is a very prestigious university and hence is much tougher to get into than the programs offered at other schools. Theey also require applications to have taken grade 12 English, advanced functions, biology, and chemistry. A competitive average mark for getting admitted here would be in the Mid to high 70s. In addition, the tuition also starts at $6,210 for local students.

  3. Brock University
    4. Brock University requires ENG4U, MHF4U, as well as SBI4U, SPH4U, SES4U and SCH4U courses. The cutoff for this program at Brock University is in the mid 70s.However, the tuition at Brock's University is extremely expensive, at $15,000 per semester.
